Deconstructing the Income Statement Waterfall

Published on August 12, 2026

If you want to break into investment banking, the Income Statement is your bread and butter. You need to know it so well that you can recite it backwards.

The Income Statement tells the story of a company’s profitability over a specific period of time. It starts with how much money the company brought in at the very top, peels away every layer of expenses, and leaves you with the actual profit at the very bottom.

1. Revenue (The Top Line)

This is the total amount of money brought in by a company’s operations. If you sell 100 widgets for $10 each, your revenue is $1,000. It doesn't matter what your costs were; this is purely the money coming through the door.

2. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

These are the direct costs attributable to the production of the goods sold by a company. For our widget company, this is the cost of the plastic, the metal, and the hourly labor on the assembly line required to make those specific widgets.

"EBITDA is a proxy for a company's core operational cash flow, ignoring the effects of debt, government taxes, and accounting rules."
