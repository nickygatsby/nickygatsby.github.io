Hello World: My Journey into Finance & Tech

Published on June 1, 2026

Documenting the hustle, learning from others, and my untraditional path to investment banking.

In the last year, I have come to realize that an excellent approach to mastering any topic is to create something, then teach it to someone else. So it makes sense that I would build this website. My name is Nikhil Binoy and I am a sophomore at UNC Chapel Hill. While many students enter corporate finance via various types of business clubs and case competitions, my path has been different. They know they want to do this from high school. I did not have a traditional business background prior to getting into finance, but over the last 2 months’ time there has been an incredible realization that I am incredibly passionate about how traditional finance and emerging technology will continue to intersect.

What Changed?

Having a "non-traditional" background was seen as disadvantageous in the beginning, but as I learned more through various experiences, I quickly came to understand that my personal "non-traditional" background became one of my best strengths. It has forced me to be very resourceful. Rather than depending on others for teaching about fundamentals, I am teaching myself by using my spare time learning to read financial statements, understand how M&A transactions work, and studying how FinTech companies are disrupting big Wall Street banks operate.

I know that IB hours and grueling and requires unrelenting work ethic and perfection. I am building this site to hold myself accountable to those standards.

The Roadmap

What is this site for? This is my public learning diary and a digital portfolio. For the next year, you’ll find me posting on three key areas:

1. Technicals: I will be sharing my own financial models (DCF’s, LBO’s, etc.) and simplifying the complex principles of accounting through post.

2. Fintech and Markets: I want to focus on the future of the industry. I will be sharing examples of innovative fintech companies, analyzing payments, and demonstrating how banks are using technology.

3. The Hustle: Getting into finance doesn’t just involve Excel, but rather includes learning through networking and development. I will document my progress towards certifications and recruiting.

"It’s not about money or connections. It’s the willingness to outwork and outlearn everyone." - Mark Cuban

To Sign Off

To any recruiters, professionals, or fellow students reading this: welcome. I am hungry to learn, eager to connect, and ready to put in the work required to succeed in this industry.
If you like fintech, investment banking, or want to talk about the markets, please don’t hesitate to connect with me on LinkedIn!
